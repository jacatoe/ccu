Intro

An Early History

The term "rock 'n' roll" is attributed to being coined by Alan Freed, a disk jockey from Cleveland. He used it to describe some of the more upbeat R&B records he played on his radio show. "Rock 'n' roll" had already been coined as a euphemism for sex, but this was the first time it was used for music. The musical origins of rock lie in African-American genres, mainly the blues. The first extremely popular rock performer was Elvis Presley, whose career began in the 1950s. Elvis opened the door for other artists such as Chuck Berry and Buddy Holly. Rock really exploded in the 1960s with the rise of British rock with icons such as The Rolling Stones, The Who, and of course The Beatles, the most popular rock band of all time. From this point onward, rock music has been a staple of music around the world.

Popularity

Rock 'n' roll is one of the most popular music genres of all time. In fact, rock can be considered the pop music of the 60s and much of the 70s. It is also one of, if not the, best selling. As evidence, the RIAA (Recording Industry Association of America) keeps a list of the best selling albums (in the US):

  1. Michal Jackson - Thriller
  2. Eagles - Their Greatest Hits
  3. Billy Joel - Greatest Hits Volume I&II
  4. Led Zeppelin - Led Zeppelin IV
  5. Pink Floyd - The Wall
  6. AC/DC - Back In Black
  7. Garth Brooks - Double Live
  8. Fleetwood Mac - Rumours
  9. Shania Twain - Come On Over
  10. The Beatles - The Beatles

As you can see, 7 of the top 10 best selling albums are considered to be in the rock genre. There are 100 albums on the list and most of them are also rock albums.
